About Me

I am a PhD student in Civil Engineering interested in numerical methods for coupled multi-physics problems, reservoir simulation, and scientific software development.

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Sciences of Civil, Environmental and Architectural Engineering at the University of Padova, under the supervision of Prof. Massimiliano Ferronato and Prof. Andrea Franceschini. My research is focused on the development of advanced numerical methods for coupled multi-physics problems in porous media, with applications to subsurface and reservoir engineering.

Since the start of my PhD, I have been one of the core developers of GReS (General Reservoir Simulator), a general-purpose computational framework for coupled multi-physics and multi-domain problems in porous media. The framework is designed to handle complex subsurface simulations involving fluid flow, geomechanics, and their interactions, using state-of-the-art discretization and coupling strategies such as mortar methods and Radial Basis Function quadrature algorithms.

GReS provides a flexible and extensible architecture for simulating coupled flow and geomechanics problems, supporting advanced discretization strategies including mortar methods for non-conforming domain decomposition and novel Radial Basis Function quadrature algorithms for accurate interface integration.
